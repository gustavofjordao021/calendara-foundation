Cozi has been the default family calendar app since the iPhone 3G era, and credit where due: it normalized the idea that a family should share one calendar. If you're comparing Cozi and Calendara in 2026, the honest summary is that they disagree about one thing, and that disagreement decides everything else.

**Cozi assumes someone will type the schedule in. Calendara assumes nobody should have to.**

## The core difference: how events get into the calendar

Every family calendar lives or dies on data entry. The school sends a year calendar with 40 dates. Camp sends a PDF. The coach sends a photo of handwriting. With Cozi, one parent transcribes all of it, event by event — which is exactly the mental-load job a family app was supposed to remove.

With Calendara, you take a photo of the flyer, the PDF, or the handwriting, and AI extracts every event on it — titles, dates, times, locations — then adds them to the shared family calendar. The 40-date school calendar takes about twenty seconds. That's not a feature difference; it's a different answer to whose job the calendar is.

Cozi does have AI now, and it's worth being precise about it: **Cozi Gold's SmartAdd** turns a line of text *you type* ("Date night Oct 28 6pm @Claire") into an event, and **Cozi Max ($79.99/year)** adds more AI. Useful — but you're still the one typing. Calendara reads the photo, and its AI is free during Early Access.

## Where each one is strong

**Calendara** — AI photo-to-calendar extraction (flyers, PDFs, screenshots, handwriting); true two-way Google Calendar sync, so events created anywhere appear everywhere; a calmer, warmer design built for glanceability; family invites that take a partner or grandparent two steps to accept; shared lists with live check-off; free during Early Access.

**Cozi** — long track record and a large user base; recipes box alongside meal planning; Android and web apps today (Calendara is iOS-first, with family members on other platforms reading via Google Calendar sync); SmartAdd and a paid AI tier if text quick-add is enough for you; brand familiarity if your school's PTA already lives on it.

## The fine print worth knowing

Cozi's free tier shows ads and now caps how far ahead you can see at 30 days; Cozi Gold runs $39/year to remove those limits, and Max is $79.99/year for the fuller AI set. Cozi's Google sync is one-direction reading in most setups — changes you make in Google don't flow back the way they do with Calendara's two-way sync. Calendara is free during Early Access, with no ads.

## Who should pick which

Pick **Cozi** if you need a native Android app today, want the recipe box, and your family already types everything in (or is happy to type a line for SmartAdd) without resentment. Pick **Calendara** if the person reading this is the person who does the typing — and would rather take a photo.

The fastest way to decide: find the most chaotic schedule in your house — the camp PDF, the team roster, the school year calendar — and photograph it with Calendara. If watching every date land on a shared calendar in one pass doesn't settle the question, Cozi will still be there.
