## ① Paste-in brief (the core block)

> **Product:** Calendara — the AI-powered family calendar. You take a photo of any schedule (school flyer, camp PDF, team roster, permission slip) and AI turns it into events on one calendar the whole family shares.
>
> **Who's talking:** a parent who carries the household's mental load — the one who keeps everyone's schedule in their head. Tired-but-warm, real, talking to camera in a car or kitchen. Not an actor reading an ad.
>
> **Format:** first-person confessional, 15–30 seconds. Raw and authentic over polished. Hook in the first 2 seconds.
>
> **The one idea, every time:** being the only one who knows the family's schedule is exhausting → you take a photo of the chaos → Calendara turns it into a shared calendar everyone can see.
>
> **Voice:** warm, plain, a little weary, relieved by the end. Short sentences. Sounds like a friend, not a brochure.
>
> **Hard rules:**
> – Never say "snap." Say "take a photo," "one photo," or "photograph it."
> – No productivity-speak: no "optimize," "time-block," "manage life smart," "hack." We help families *coordinate so they can be together* — never "optimize their lives."
> – No exclamation points / no hype delivery.
> – End on the product move + name: "…it's called Calendara" or "link in bio."

**C · Co-parent / partner**

**D · The relief**
> "I didn't realize how much space the family schedule was taking up in my head until it wasn't there anymore. Take a photo, it's on the calendar, everyone can see it. I close the app and I'm just… here. With them."

## ⑦ Content Angle library (Title / Description / Target Audience)

Each maps to a brand pillar. Paste into the "Add Angle" form. Every angle resolves the same way: take a photo → shared family calendar.

**Mental Load Overwhelm** *(have it — Pillar: Mental Load)*
- *Description:* The invisible full-time job of being the only one who knows the family's schedule. Name the weight first (school emails, soccer texts, permission slips living in one head), then the relief: take a photo of any of it and it becomes a calendar everyone can see.
- *Audience:* The "default parent" who carries the whole household's schedule in their head — usually mom.

**Missed Event Anxiety** *(have it — Pillar: Mental Load)*
- *Description:* The low-grade dread of dropping something — the forgotten dress-up day, the RSVP, the dentist move. Calendara catches what you'd otherwise have to remember: photograph the flyer and the date is on everyone's calendar with reminders.
- *Audience:* Parents who've been burned by a forgotten school event or appointment and live a little afraid of the next one.

**Manual Planning Burnout** *(have it — Pillar: The Magic)*
- *Description:* The grind of retyping schedules by hand — copying a school-year PDF into your phone, event by event. Calendara removes the typing entirely: one photo, AI reads the whole thing.
- *Audience:* Parents who transcribe school/camp/team schedules into their calendar manually.

**The Photo Fix** *(add — Pillar: The Magic / wedge)*
- *Description:* The core demo. A flyer, a PDF, a screenshot, even handwriting → take one photo and watch it become real events on a shared calendar. Show the magic, don't just describe it.
- *Audience:* Parents drowning in paper — flyers on the fridge, PDFs in the inbox, permission slips in the backpack.

**Camp & Summer Schedule Chaos** *(add — seasonal, Pillar: The Magic)*
- *Description:* Multiple kids, multiple camps, multiple paper schedules, all summer. Photograph each one and every drop-off, swim day and early pickup lands on a calendar the whole family shares.
- *Audience:* Parents juggling more than one kid's camps and summer activities.

**Partner Can't See It** *(add — Pillar: Shared/co-parent)*
- *Description:* The fight that was never about the calendar — it was that one person was the only one who knew what was on it ("I told you about the recital… in March"). Share the calendar, split the seeing. Works for two-parent and co-parenting/divorced households.
- *Audience:* Co-parents and partners where one person owns the schedule and the other feels out of the loop.

**Close the App, Be Present** *(add — Pillar: The Feeling)*
- *Description:* The payoff, not the product. Once the schedule is out of your head and on a shared calendar, you can stop managing and just be there. Calm, warm, present — close the app, be with them.
- *Audience:* Burned-out parents craving presence over productivity.
