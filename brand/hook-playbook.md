# Calendara Hook Playbook — the 6 hook types, reproduced

Source: Simon Robert (@Simon__Rob), "6 hook types that consistently work on Meta" (10k ads/mo). These are **paid Meta-ad** hooks, but the principle — *win the first frame/line* — is identical for our **organic** TikTok/IG. (Paid is parked anyway: no budget + the CLND-221 attribution gap.)

## The big one for us: Pattern Interrupt → go RAW
> "The feed is trained on polish, so unpolished gets noticed first."

We've been polishing Storybook-Glow content for days, and the data says it **reaches but doesn't stop the scroll or convert** (≈2s video watch, ~0 saves/follows). This is the likely reason. **The hook/first frame should be deliberately unpolished; the *payoff* (demo, CTA) stays clean.** Raw hook → polished proof.

---

## 1. Pattern interrupt — a visual that doesn't fit the feed
Unpolished, "made on a phone," not branded. The scroll-stopper.

**Calendara visuals (shoot raw, no Glow):**
- A real, unstyled phone photo of **a fridge buried in flyers/magnets** → text: "this is my family's 'calendar' 🙃"
- A **pile of crumpled school papers** on the counter → "the school sent 6 of these this week"
- A **Notes-app / Reddit-style screenshot**: "not sure who needs this but being the family calendar is a full-time job no one sees"
- A **handwritten/crayon weekly schedule**, messy and crossed-out

**Format:** raw single image, or slide 1 of a Photo Mode slideshow, or first 1s of a reel — then cut to the clean demo/payoff.
**Channel:** **TikTok FYP first** (pattern interrupt is built for the cold scroll); also works as an IG Reel cover.

## 2. Direct address — "If you're [specific situation], read this"
Speaks to one person; qualifies in 2 seconds. (We've used this and it's on-brand.)

**Calendara hooks:**
- "If you're the only one who knows the soccer schedule — this is for you."
- "If your family calendar lives in your head, read this."
- "For the parent who remembers every practice, party, and pickup 👇"

**Format:** text hook (HookReel beat 1 / slideshow cover / pure text post).
**Channel:** **both** — strongest on **IG** (relatable → DM **sends**, IG's reach currency) and a solid TikTok hook.

## 3. Diagnostic question — the brain can't help but answer
Diagnoses, doesn't sell.

**Calendara hooks:**
- "Be honest — how many school emails are unread right now?"
- "When did your partner last know the schedule without asking you?"
- "Why do *you* remember the dentist appointment and no one else does?"

**Format:** **text post (zero production)**, or HookReel hook / slideshow cover.
**Channel:** **both** — cheap, infinite text posts; great on TikTok and IG.

## 4. Confession — founder-tone, admits what a brand wouldn't
No face needed — works as text-on-screen / founder caption.

**Calendara hooks (your voice):**
- "I build a family calendar app. My wife still runs 90% of our family's schedule."
- "I made a calendar app and *still* forgot my kid's water-play day."
- "We almost cut the photo feature from Calendara. It's now the only reason people stay."

**Format:** founder text post, or text-on-screen confession over a raw clip.
**Channel:** **TikTok** (confession/founder tone is native there); cross-post to IG.

## 5. Offer-led — a specific number in the first frame
Data stops the scroll; the more specific, the more credible. **Use REAL numbers** — pull actuals before posting (avg events per photo, processing seconds, time saved). Don't invent.

**Calendara hooks (verify each):**
- "1 photo → [N] events on your calendar."
- "Calendara reads a school flyer in ~[N] seconds."
- "Stop hand-typing ~[N] school events a month."

**Format:** data hook over a clean frame — and the **screen-rec demo is inherently offer-led** (it shows the speed/result). Pair them.
**Channel:** **both**; strongest paired with the demo (number + proof).

## 6. Contrarian claim — contradicts a held belief
The brain can't scroll past a challenge to what it believes.

**Calendara hooks:**
- "A shared calendar won't fix your mental load."
- "You're not disorganized. You're just the only one who knows."
- "Color-coding won't save you — a second brain will."
- "Family calendars don't fail because you're messy. They fail because they live in one head."

**Format:** text post / HookReel hook / slideshow cover.
**Channel:** **both** — contrarian is a strong scroll-stop on the TikTok FYP.

---

## How this maps to our formats + channels
- **Hook type = the first frame/line.** It rides on top of our existing formats: HookReel (video), Photo Mode slideshow, pure text post, or the screen-rec demo.
- **TikTok (our reach surface):** pattern interrupt · diagnostic · contrarian · confession — built for the cold FYP.
- **IG (reels for reach, carousels for sends):** direct address · confession — relatable, drive sends.
- **The demo is the universal payoff:** any raw hook → cut to the clean photo→calendar proof → App Store CTA.

## What to test first
1. **A raw Pattern-Interrupt hook** (real messy fridge/flyer photo or a Notes-app confession) → cut to the clean demo. This is the highest-information test because it directly challenges the "too polished" hypothesis behind our flat conversion.
2. **One Diagnostic and one Contrarian text post** (zero production) — cheap, and we've never tried these angles.
3. Keep the polished demo/CTA as the payoff behind whichever hook wins.
