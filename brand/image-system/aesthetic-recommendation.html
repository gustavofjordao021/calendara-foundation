The recommendation

Lead#3 Storybook painterly+ #1 hand-drawn line as the supporting texture layer

It’s the only candidate that delivers your voice in a single frame. Storybook painterly is warmth, togetherness, and memory — it maps directly onto the “keepsake, not a to-do list” vision and the undesigned “Glow.” It also breaks hardest from both Cozi’s dated look and the flat-SaaS sameness you’re standing in. It scored highest on the two criteria that are pure brand (emotional register, differentiation) and on palette — it already lives in your warm tones.

  • Pair it with #1 (recolored warm) as a hand-drawn line/texture layer for hero backdrops, section dividers, blog headers and empty states. It extends the one hand-made element you already ship (the rough callouts) and is far cheaper to scale than full painterly scenes.
  • Keep the v3.1 UI + wash + callout system for anything that explains the product. Illustration carries the feeling; the screenshot carries the proof. Don’t illustrate what you can show.
  • Reserve #4 (cinematic) for a one-off launch/brand film. Pass on #2 (isometric riso) — it’s the coolest, busiest, least-hygge of the set.

Do — how to make #3 yours

  • Recolor to the tokens: coral #C96442, sand #F2E4D8 / #FFF8F2, sage #5B8C3F, gold; espresso #3D2418 for line & shadow; dusty blue as the only cool note.
  • Families together, diverse configs (mandated), in domestic/evening settings with sensory props — table, candle, basket, plants — that echo the Hygge nouns.
  • Make warm light the hero. That glow is your brand’s “Glow.”
  • Keep it grown-up: a modern picture-book illustrator, not Saturday-morning cartoon. The reader is the adult “family manager.”
  • Lock a style kit (palette + prompt + 6–8 master scenes) so a solo + AI workflow stays on-model.

Don’t

  • Don’t go saturated or busy — calm & breathable wins; one quiet focal moment, not a crowded scene.
  • Don’t force painterly onto feature shots — it can’t show the UI.
  • Don’t let it read childish — that loses the credibility Cozi-switchers need.
  • Don’t over-commit budget before a small real-world read (below).
Copyright guardrail. Your references for #3 and #4 are Pikachu and a Pixar-style still — useful for style only. Any adopted system must use original characters and scenes. Do not generate Pokémon, Disney/Pixar characters, or any other studio’s IP, in production or in tests.

Verification & honest caveats

How this was checked. Every brand claim is grounded in something I actually read: the v3.1 token board (CLND-293/296), the parsed calendara.pen (Manrope ramp, coral #C96442 at 1,090 uses, warm-only palette), the live site, and the Hygge copy (“close the app, be with them”). The four candidates were scored on the same eight rubric items derived from those sources.

What this is not. It’s a brand-fit judgment, not an A/B test. Before you spend on a full library: commission 2–3 real hero comps in the #3 direction (warm-recolored), drop them beside a current App Store frame, and get a quick gut-read from a few ICP power users (e.g., Apryl). The biggest practical risk isn’t fit — it’s keeping a painterly style consistent as a solo operator; the locked style kit + reusing master scenes is the mitigation. If consistency proves hard, lean more on the #1 line layer, which tolerates variance far better.

Bottom line: Lead with storybook-painterly warmth for the emotional 20% (hero, social, onboarding, memory features), back it with warm hand-drawn line for the everyday, and keep your crisp UI + wash + callout system for the functional 80%. Make the pictures finally feel like the words.
