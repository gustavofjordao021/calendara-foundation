White space — what nobody does

1 · No animated product demo, anywhere

Zero autoplaying UI walkthroughs across all six. Skylight outsources demos to live webinars; Maple shows an input, not an outcome; Hearth's video is brand, not UI. A photo→events demo in the hero is completely unclaimed — and it's our #1 differentiator (27% feature adoption; photo posts convert at 38–41%). The ai-demo-web/mobile loops are, as of today, a category first.

2 · Nobody solves the desktop→phone handoff

No QR, no text-me-a-link on any site. The apps dodge it with web signup (Cozi, TimeTree) — we can't and never will (iOS-only, no web app), so our QR/text-me pattern isn't just parity, it's the only honest solution in the category — and 58% of our traffic needs it.

3 · Nobody owns warm illustration

Flat vector (TimeTree) and pen doodles (Maple) exist; painterly-emotional does not. Skylight is the only warm-palette kin and it's photography-based hardware. Storybook Glow has no competitor.

4 · Nobody depicts the "before"

Every site describes the chaos villain in words; none show it. Our fridge-flyer-chaos Problem image is another unclaimed visual.

5 · Threat clock

Skylight's Sidekick (paper/PDF/email→events, 1M+ families) is the real feature threat, currently buried as a bullet; Maple shipped photo ingestion and doesn't market it. The demo-forward window is open but should be assumed temporary.

What to adopt (they do it better today)

Skylight's proof stack — named testimonials with hometowns, trust tiles, counts. We can't match the numbers yet; we can match the grammar: real named quote (Apryl candidate), founder story (already on /about), trust tiles (private-by-design · two-way Google sync · free during Early Access). Keep proof identical across breakpoints (Cozi hides its best numbers from desktop — don't inherit that bug). Hearth's outcome framing — kid/family outcomes over features; our "Less logistics. More life." section carries this, keep it prominent. Skylight's mental-load quiz — strong lead-magnet + SEO idea for the photo cluster; backlog candidate, not launch-blocking. TimeTree's price honesty — we already shout free; keep it unmissable.

Verdict: redesign confirmed — v2.1 is differentiated on every axis that matters. Build it.

Should we? Yes — but the scan's real finding is that this isn't a "catch up" redesign, it's a "claim the white space" redesign. The current live site competes in the same grammar as Cozi/FamilyWall (describe features, show badges). v2.1 as designed occupies four unclaimed positions at once: animated photo→AI demo (nobody), warm painterly emotional register (nobody), depicted before-chaos (nobody), honest desktop→phone handoff (nobody, and we uniquely need it).

How? No direction change. Priorities sharpened by the scan: (1) the demo loop is the centerpiece — ship it with the blog template first, homepage second; (2) borrow Skylight's proof grammar within our truth constraints; (3) keep the outcome section loud; (4) move before Skylight markets Sidekick or Maple shows its ingestion — the window is the strategy.

Positioning line the matrix supports: For the parent who keeps track of everything, Calendara is the family calendar that fills itself from a photo — warm where Maple is cold, honest-scale where Cozi is legacy, software-priced where Skylight is $300 hardware.
